Mapping Healthcare Access with the Allegheny County Health Department

An opportunity offered by the CMU Statistics Department

Overview

For the duration of the fall semester of 2024, I worked with the Allegheny County Health Department and the Carnegie Mellon University Statistics Department on a public health data analysis project. Our initial focus was on trend analysis and forecasting of Hepatitis C cases across the county using historical surveillance data. However, we were limited to relatively small datasets for Allegheny County, which made it difficult to build reliable predictive models. Beyond our preliminary trend and other exploratory analysis, the lack of sufficient data led us to reconsider the scope of the project. This early phase still allowed me to develop skills in data cleaning, statistical modeling in R, and thinking critically about data limitations in real-world public health contexts.

Given these constraints, we transitioned the project toward analyzing geographic disparities in healthcare access throughout the county. I took the lead on researching and identifying supplemental datasets from national resources such as the CDC and the Health Resources & Services Administration (HRSA), which helped us expand our focus. Using this data, I mapped the distribution of emergency rooms, opioid treatment centers, and federally qualified health centers to assess gaps in access to care. Through this work, I applied geospatial analysis in R to highlight underserved regions and gain insight into how structural inequities shape public health outcomes. This shift in focus not only made our work more impactful but also emphasized the value of adaptability and independent research in applied statistics projects.

Tools Used

  • R
  • ggplot2
  • Data scavenging and cleaning
