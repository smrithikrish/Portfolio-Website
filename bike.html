BikePGH x SUDS

A collaborative project through CMU's club Students Using Data for Social Good and the nonprofit BikePGH

Overview

As part of a public interest data project, we conducted a comprehensive spatial analysis of bike infrastructure in Pittsburgh using data from BikePGH. The project focused on evaluating the accessibility and equity of the city’s bike network by integrating multiple data sources and visualizing key infrastructure trends.

Using R, we mapped existing bike lanes, identified the most frequently used paths, and examined newly added lanes—particularly those that coincided with road widening projects. We also analyzed the distribution of public bike racks and stand locations, assessing how well they aligned with high-traffic routes and residential density.

This project strengthened my geospatial data skills and gave me hands-on experience with equity-focused urban planning. It also underscored the importance of integrating public data and visualization tools to inform policy and infrastructure decisions in local communities.

Tools Used

  • R
  • ArcGIS
